Sustainability

Fresh thinking from the ground up

New standards for responsible living

We create homes that not only save energy but also actively contribute to a carbon-neutral future, setting new standards for environmentally responsible living.

Our homes are better sealed and insulated to reduce the energy needed for cooling and heating. Smart building controls manage cooling and lighting more efficiently, while our roofs feature integrated solar panels to generate power directly in each home.

Sustainable by nature

Renewable

Wood is a fantastic and fully renewable resource.

CO2 binding

Trees bind atmospheric carbon through photosynthesis as they grow.

Energy-efficient

Wood is an excellent insulator that does not retain heat or cold.

Eco-friendly
by design

Our homes are built with efficiency and sustainability in mind.

Resource-conscious design

We create homes that harness innovation to do more with fewer valuable resources.

Energy-saving construction

Our homes capture and store CO2 in their timber frames so we can lower the carbon footprint of home construction by over 80%.

Energy-efficient homes

Our homes use around 40% less energy over their lifetime than a traditional house built from brick or concrete.

We source from responsibly-managed, FSC‑certified forests

Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certified forests adhere to strict environmental, social, and economic standards. The wood we source is all FSC‑certified. The certification confirms that the forest is being managed in a way that preserves biological diversity and benefits the lives of local people and workers, while ensuring it sustains economic viability.

Supporting Net Zero by 2050

The UAE is the first MENA nation to commit to net-zero emissions by 2050. This aligns with the Paris Agreement goal of reducing greenhouse gas, and the Principles of the 50, which will transform The Emirates into the world’s most dynamic economy.

Our construction methods can help provide many of the new homes needed for regional growth, while enabling the UAE’s ambitious CO2 reduction targets.
